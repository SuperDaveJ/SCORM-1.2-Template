14 CFR, Part 21, Subpart H, Airworthiness Certificates

14 CFR, Part 21, Subpart H, Airworthiness Certificates is the regulatory authority for issuing Airworthiness Certificates.

14 CFR, Part 43, Maintenance, Rebuilding, and Alteration

14 CFR, Part 43, Maintenance, Rebuilding, and Alteration prescribes the rules governing the maintenance, preventive maintenance, rebuilding, and alteration for aircraft holding a U.S. Airworthiness Certificate (with some exceptions).

14 CFR, Part 45, Identification and Registration Marking

14 CFR, Part 45, Identification and Registration Marking contains the identification and national registration marking requirements for type certificated aircraft, aircraft engines, and propellers.

14 CFR, Part 47, Aircraft Registration

14 CFR, Part 47, Aircraft Registration requires an aircraft be registered in the U.S. to its legal owner before it can be certificated or operated.

14 CFR, Part 91, General Operating and Flight Rules

14 CFR, Part 91, General Operating and Flight Rules prescribes the rules governing the operation of aircraft. Also included are the requirements for the owner/operator of an aircraft to maintain an aircraft in an airworthy condition. This includes inspections and maintenance of the aircraft and associated equipment as well as maintenance record keeping requirements.

AC 45-2, Identification and Registration Marking

AC 45-2, Identification and Registration Marking provides guidance for applying identification and registration marks. It describes how to comply with the requirements for identifying aircraft, aircraft engines, and propellers to meet 14 CFR, Part 45.

Bilateral Airworthiness Agreement (BAA)

A Bilateral Airworthiness Agreement (BAA) is an executive agreement concluded prior to 1996 through an exchange of diplomatic notes between the U.S. State Department and its foreign counterparts based on FAA technical recommendations.

Bilateral Aviation Safety Agreements (BASAs)

Bilateral Aviation Safety Agreements (BASAs) facilitate bilateral cooperation in a variety of aviation areas, including maintenance, flight operations, and environmental certification.

Blue ribbon package

A blue ribbon package is a package certified for use in an enforcement action.

Electronic Document Retrieval System (EDRS)

Electronic Document Retrieval System (EDRS) houses the official Airworthiness Certification and Registration records.

Exemption

Exemptions allow a petitioner relief, in part or in whole, from the requirements of a specific CFR section or paragraph. The exemption request is treated like any other regulation and it goes through nearly the same process. When and if it is granted, it becomes the law, at least for that petitioner. An exemption from the regulation must go through the Notice of Proposed Rulemaking (NPRM) process.

Experimental Certificates

Experimental Certificates may be issued for specific purposes including: research and development, showing compliance with regulations, crew training, exhibition, air racing, market surveys, operating amateur-built aircraft, operating primary kit-built aircraft, operating light-sport aircraft.

FAA Advisory Circulars (ACs)

FAA Advisory Circulars (ACs) contain non-regulatory material on policy, guidance, and general information. They do not have the force of law. Some Advisory Circulars (ACs) may provide guidance on methods acceptable to the administrator for compliance with parts and sections of 14 CFR.

FAA Form 8130-3

FAA Form 8130-3 is a multi-purpose form that is used for several Airworthiness Certification functions.

FAA Order 8130.2, Airworthiness Certification of Aircraft and Related Products

FAA Order 8130.2, Airworthiness Certification of Aircraft and Related Products is the primary document for Airworthiness Certification guidance. It describes the procedures for issuing original and recurrent Standard Airworthiness Certificates, including detailed instructions for completing the applicable forms.

Implementation Procedures for Airworthiness (IPAs)

Implementation Procedures for Airworthiness (IPAs) include the procedures developed to address specific areas such as design approvals, production activities, export airworthiness approval, and post-design approval activities.

Import

When a foreign manufactured aircraft is manufactured to a U.S. TC and is exported from a country with whom the U.S. has a bilateral agreement and accompanied by an Export Airworthiness Approval from a bilateral CAA it is considered an import. This could be the country of manufacture, or a third country with whom we have third party provisions in the bilateral agreement

National policy

National policy is policy found in statements giving guidance or acceptable practices for complying with specific CFR sections or paragraphs. National policy is policy that is normally issued by FAA headquarters. National policy includes: orders, advisory circulars (ACs), notices, and policy memorandums.

New engine, propeller, or article

An new engine, propeller, or article is an engine, propeller, or article with no time in service. The phrase “time in service” is defined in 14 CFR, Part 1 as the time from the moment the aircraft leaves the surface of the earth until it touches at the next point of landing. This does not include time accrued during functional testing performed on the ground.

Program letter

An applicant must submit a program letter stating the purpose of the experiment and outlining the program objectives. The letter must be detailed enough to permit the FAA to prescribe the conditions and limitations necessary to ensure safe operation of the aircraft. A new program letter will be required when significant changes to the aircraft configuration or program objectives are planned.

Registration markings

Registration markings shall be displayed either on the vertical tail surfaces or the sides of the fuselage. If displayed on the vertical tail surfaces, the marks can be vertical or horizontal.

Regulatory and Guidance Library (RGL)

The Regulatory and Guidance Library (RGL) is the primary source for the most current reference material for performing Airworthiness Certifications.

Replacement Airworthiness Certificate

A Replacement Airworthiness Certificate is issued when a certificate is declared lost, has been mutilated, is no longer legible, or contains inaccurate and/or erroneous information.

Safety Performance Analysis System (SPAS)

Safety Performance Analysis System (SPAS) is an analysis tool that brings information from various databases together into one all-inclusive source.

Standard Airworthiness Certificates

A Standard Airworthiness Certificates is issued for aircraft that are FAA type certificated. Each type certificate is considered to include the type design, the Operating Limitations, and the Type Certificate Data Sheet (TCDS), or Aircraft Specification document. Aircraft Specifications were issued to older aircraft before the use of TCDS.

Title 49 USC, Section 44701

Title 49 USC, Section 44701 states that the FAA Administrator shall promote safe flight of civil aircraft in air commerce by prescribing minimum standards for design, material, and performance of aircraft, aircraft engines, propellers, and appliances.

Title 49 USC, Section 44702(a)

Title 49 USC, Section 44702(a) gives the administrator the authority to Airman Certificates, Type Certificates, Production Certificates, Airworthiness Certificates, Air Carrier Operating Certificates, Airport Operating Certificates, Air Agency Certificates, and Air Navigation Facility Certificates.

Used engine, propeller, or article

An engine, propeller, or article with any amount of time in service is considered used. The phrase “time in service” is defined in 14 CFR Part 1 as the time from the moment the aircraft leaves the surface of the earth until it touches at the next point of landing. This does not include time accrued during functional testing performed on the ground.
