Marking of Aircraft

A manufacturer of aircraft, covered under 14 CFR Section 21.182, must mark each aircraft by attaching a fireproof plate that includes the information specified in 14 CFR Section 45.13.

Per 14 CFR Section 45.11, the required ID plate may be secured to the aircraft exterior, viewable from the ground, except for aircraft produced for:

  • Operations under Part 121
  • Commuter operations
  • Export
  • Aircraft operating under Part 121 and under an FAA-approved Continuous Airworthiness Maintenance Program (CAMP)
  • Aircraft operating under commuter air carrier operations (as defined in 119.3) under an FAA-approved CAMP
  • Gliders

For powered parachutes and weight-shift control aircraft, the ID plate must be secured to the exterior of the fuselage so that it is legible to a person on the ground.
