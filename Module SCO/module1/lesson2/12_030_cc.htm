The statement “aircraft manufactured to other than U.S. requirements imported to the U.S.” means a foreign manufactured aircraft originally exported to a country other than the U.S. and there is no Export C of A to USA stating compliance to an FAA TC. This would be an original certification for an import aircraft and there are no certification procedures for this situation in our guidance.

For example, suppose an airbus was built in France and sold new and exported to an airline in Australia, so the aircraft was manufactured to Australian requirements. Years later, the same aircraft was sold by the Australian airline to a U.S. customer. That aircraft would be coming to the U.S. from Australia without an export from either France to the U.S. or Australia to the U.S. (third party).

The process of importing foreign manufactured aircraft to the U.S. includes the issuance of an export approval from the CAA of either the country of manufacturer or a third country with whom the U.S. has a bilateral agreement with third party provisions for acceptance of that export approval. Therefore, all properly imported aircraft will be a recurrent certification to issue the first Airworthiness Certificate in the U.S.
